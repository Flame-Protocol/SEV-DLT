Lesson 1: What Is an Entitlement?

Before we talk about technology, blockchains, or anything complicated, let's start with a word that might sound fancy but actually describes something very simple: entitlement.

The Simple Definition

An entitlement is a right to receive something. That's it. Nothing more complicated.

If someone owes you something ‚Äî money, goods, services, or anything else ‚Äî you have an entitlement. It's your right to get it.

üí° Real Life Examples

You already deal with entitlements every day:

  • A gift card ‚Äî entitles you to $50 worth of stuff from a store
  • A plane ticket ‚Äî entitles you to a seat on a specific flight
  • A paycheck ‚Äî entitles you to receive money from your employer
  • A receipt for repair ‚Äî entitles you to get your fixed item back

Why Is This Important?

The FLAME Protocol creates a new kind of entitlement ‚Äî one that's digital, permanent, and impossible to take away.

Instead of a paper receipt that can be lost, or a database entry that can be deleted, your entitlement is recorded in a way that no one can alter or erase.

üîë Key Takeaway

An entitlement is simply your right to receive something. The FLAME Protocol makes those rights permanent and provable.

A sovereign entity is one that governs itself. It doesn't answer to a higher authority for its internal decisions.
